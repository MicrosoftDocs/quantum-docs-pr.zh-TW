# <a name="what-is-quantum-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="82be8-103">什麼是量子運算？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-103">What is quantum computing?</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-104">世界上有某些問題極為困難，數量上不可思議的龐大，因此即使讓世界上的每一部超級電腦聯手處理這個問題，仍然要花上比宇宙的壽命還要長的時間才能解決。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-104">There are some problems so difficult, so incredibly vast, that even if every supercomputer in the world worked on the problem in tandem it would still take longer than the lifetime of the universe to solve.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-105">量子電腦有望解決地球上的某些最大挑戰，環境方面、農業方面、健康方面、能源方面、氣候方面、材料科學方面，以及我們從未想到的各種方面。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-105">Quantum computers hold the promise to solve some of our planet's biggest challenges - in environment, agriculture, health, energy, climate, materials science and those we’ve not yet even imagined.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-106">量子電腦的影響將會非常深遠，其影響程度和 1947 年時，因為創造出電晶體，而成就了現今的數位經濟是一樣的。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-106">The impact of quantum computers will be far-reaching and have as great an impact as the creation of the transistor in 1947, which paved the way for today’s digital economy.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-107">量子運算會利用量子物理的獨特行為，來提供全新且功能巨大的運算模型。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-107">Quantum computing harnesses the unique behavior of quantum physics to provide a new and very powerful model of computing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-108">量子物理的理論認為，量子級別的物質可以疊加多個傳統狀態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-108">The theory of quantum physics posits that matter, at a quantum level can be in a superposition of multiple classical states.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-109">這些狀態會彼此干擾，就和潮汐池中的波浪一樣。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-109">And those many states interfere with each other like waves in a tide pool.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-110">假定測量之後物質的狀態會「塌縮」為其中一個古典狀態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-110">The state of matter after a measurement is assumed to "collapse" into one of the classical states.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-111">之後，重複相同的測量將產生相同的古典結果。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-111">Thereafter, repeating the same measurement will produce the same classical result.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-112">當粒子間的互動會讓各自的量子狀態無法完全獨立於其他粒子單獨描述，即使這些粒子實際上距離遙遠，就會發生量子纏結。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-112">Quantum entanglement occurs when particles interact in ways such that the quantum state of each cannot be completely described independently of the others, even if the particles are physically far apart.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-113">量子運算會以物質的量子狀態儲存資訊，並使用量子的疊加和纏結本質透過計算該資訊實行量子操作，進而利用並學習量子干擾的程式設計。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-113">Quantum computing stores information in quantum states of matter and use its quantum nature of superposition and entanglement to realize quantum operations that compute on that information, thereby harnessing and learning to program quantum interference.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-114">量子運算聽起來可能會讓人退避三舍，但只要有適當的資源，您馬上就可以開始建置量子應用程式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-114">Quantum computing might sound daunting, but with the right resources you can start building quantum applications today.</span></span>

## <a name="the-qubit"></a><span data-ttu-id="82be8-115">量子位元</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-115">The qubit</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-116">量子運算定義了反映量子行為的運算概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-116">Quantum computing defines computing concepts that reflect the quantum behavior.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-117">量子運算從量子位元的概念開始。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-117">Quantum computing begins with the notion of a qubit.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-118">在量子運算中，量子位元 (**qubit**) 是量子資訊的單位，就和傳統位元一樣。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-118">In quantum computing, a quantum bit - **qubit** - is a unit of quantum information, like a classical bit.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-119">其中，古典位元會有單一二進位值，例如 0 或 1，而量子位元的狀態則可以同時是 0 和 1 的**疊加**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-119">Where classical bits hold a single binary value such as a 0 or 1, the state of a qubit can be in a **superposition** of 0 and 1 simultaneously.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-120">測量量子位元的舉動會改變量子位元的狀態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-120">The act of measuring a qubit changes a qubit state.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-121">測量時，量子位元會從疊加狀態變為兩個古典狀態的其中一個。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-121">With measurement, the qubit goes from being in superposition to one of the classical states.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-122">多個量子位元也可以相互**纏結**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-122">Multiple qubits can also be **entangled**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-123">當我們測量一個纏結的量子位元時，對其他量子位元狀態的了解也會隨之更新。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-123">When we make a measurement of one entangled qubit, our knowledge of the state of the other(s) is updated as well.</span></span>

## <a name="quantum-algorithms"></a><span data-ttu-id="82be8-124">量子演算法</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-124">Quantum algorithms</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-125">量子演算法是設計成利用量子的本質和行為來加速古典演算法，也可以設計成提供全新的實體系統建模方式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-125">Quantum algorithms are designed to take advantage of quantum nature and behavior to speed up classical algorithms, or to provide entirely new ways of modeling physical systems.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-126">這些演算法會利用量子位元資訊的編碼方式，以及對疊加狀態中多個纏結的量子位元進行操作的平行特性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-126">These algorithms exploit the the way qubits encode information and the parallel nature of operating on multiple entangled qubits in superposition.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-127">古典電腦以位元為單位將資訊編碼；每個位元編碼有 2 個可能的值，0 或 1。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-127">Classical computers encode information in bits; each bit encoding 2 possible values, 0 or 1.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-128">一個量子位元會同時對 0 和 1 兩個值進行編碼。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-128">One qubit encodes two values simultaneously, 0 and 1.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-129">兩個古典位元的編碼是 4 個可能值 (00、01、10、11) 的其中一個，而 2 個量子位元的編碼則是這 4 個值同時存在的任何疊加，雖然我們在測量時只會得到這些值的其中一個。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-129">Two classical bits encode one of 4 possible values, (00, 01, 10, 11) whereas 2 qubits encodes any superposition of those 4 simultaneously, although we can obtain ony one of those values when measuring.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-130">四個古典位元的編碼是 16 個值的其中一個。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-130">Four bits encode one of 16 values.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-131">四個量子位元的編碼則是16 個值同時存在的任何疊加，依此類推，呈指數增加。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-131">Four qubits encode any superposition of 16 values simultaneously, and so on, exponentially.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="82be8-132">100 個量子位元可以編碼的資訊，比現今最大的電腦系統能提供的還要多。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-132">100 qubits can encode more information than is available in the largest computer systems today.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-133">除此之外，當多個纏結的量子位元以相干方式行動時，便可同時處理多個選項。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-133">Furthermore, when multiple entangled qubits act coherently, they can process multiple options simultaneously.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-134">這會讓這些量子位元在處理資訊時，所需時間遠遠少於最快的非量子系統。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-134">This allows them to process information in a fraction of the time it would take even the fastest non-quantum systems.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-135">運用這些量子特性已經是量子演算法研究數十年來追求的目標，而且已經找到許多可以解決問題的創新技術，花費的時間更是解決傳統方式的數分之一。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-135">Harnessing these quantum attributes has been the pursuit of multiple decades of quantum algorithm research, and there are many innovative techniques that have been found that solve problems in a fraction of the time it takes to solve classically.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-136">最著名的量子演算法之一是用來進行因數分解的「Shor 演算法」  ，此演算法讓傳統上非常棘手，要將巨大數字因數分解為兩個質數的問題，變得很快就能得出結果，因此讓傳統的密碼編譯受到挑戰。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-136">One of the most famous quantum algorithms is _Shor's algorithm_ for factorization, which makes the classically intractable problem of factorization of a large number into two prime numbers fast enough to challenge traditional cryptography.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-137">從比較積極的角度來看，可供安全散發密碼編譯金鑰的演算法會因為量子位元的疊加、量子糾纏和**不可複製** (也就是，無法在不被偵測到的情況下複製量子位元) 特性而有可能成真。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-137">On the more constructive side, algorithms for secure cryptographic key distribution are made possible by superposition, quantum entanglement, and the **no cloning** property of qubits, meaning the inability for qubits to be copied without detection.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-138">_格羅弗演算法_強調一種量子演算法技術，可讓您在搜尋非結構化資料時進行二次加速。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-138">_Grover's algorithm_ highlights a quantum algorithm technique that provides a quadratic speed-up for searching unstructured data.</span></span>


## <a name="quantum-hardware"></a><span data-ttu-id="82be8-139">量子硬體</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-139">Quantum hardware</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-140">在傳統電腦中，位元會對應到晶片電路中的電壓位準。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-140">In classical computers, bits correspond to voltage levels in silicon circuits.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-141">量子運算硬體可透過許多不同的量子位元表現形式來實現：捕獲離子、超導性質、中性原子、電子自旋、光偏極化、拓撲量子位元。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-141">Quantum computing hardware can be implemented by many different physical realizations of qubits: trapped ions, superconducting, neutral atoms, electron spin, light polarization, topological qubits.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-142">量子硬體是一種新興技術。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-142">Quantum hardware is an emergent technology.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-143">量子位元天生就很脆弱，並且會因為與環境互動而讓相干程度變差。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-143">Qubits are fragile by nature and become less coherent as they interact with their environment.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-144">這需要透過延展性來平衡系統的準確性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-144">This requires balancing fidelity of the system with scalability.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-145">規模越大 (也就是量子位元數目越多)，錯誤率就會越高。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-145">The larger the scale (that is, number of qubits), the higher the error rate.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="82be8-146">Microsoft 正在根據拓撲量子位元來開發量子電腦。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-146">Microsoft is developing a quantum computer based on topological qubits.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-147">我們認為拓撲量子位元較不會受到其環境變更所影響，因此可減少外部糾錯程度。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-147">We believe a topological qubit will be less impacted by changes in its environment, therefore reducing the degree of external error correction.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="82be8-148">拓撲量子位元提高了穩定性和對抗環境雜訊的能力，這表示它們可以更輕鬆地調整規模，並長期保持可靠。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="82be8-148">Topological qubits feature increased stability and resistance to environmental noise, which means they can more readily scale and remain reliable longer.</span></span>
