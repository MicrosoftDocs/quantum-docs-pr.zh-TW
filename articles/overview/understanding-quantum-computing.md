# <a name="understanding-quantum-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-103">了解量子計算</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-103">Understanding quantum computing</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-104">量子運算會使用量子力學原理來處理資訊。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-104">Quantum computing uses the principles of quantum mechanics to process information.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-105">因此，量子運算所需的方法與傳統運算不同。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-105">Because of this, quantum computing requires a different approach than classical computing.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="6462a-106">這項差異的其中一個範例是量子電腦中所使用的處理器。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-106">One example of this difference is the processor used in quantum computers.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="6462a-107">傳統電腦使用的是我們熟悉的矽基晶片，量子電腦所使用的則是量子材料，例如原子、離子、光子或電子。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-107">Where classical computers use familiar silicon-based chips, quantum computers use quantum materials such as atoms, ions, photons, or electrons.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-108">量子材料的行為會遵循量子力學定律，因此會利用概率運算、疊加和糾纏等概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-108">The quantum material behaves according to the laws of quantum mechanics, leveraging concepts such as probabilistic computation, superposition, and entanglement.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-109">這些概念為量子演算法提供了基礎，讓其可以充分運用量子運算的威力來解決複數問題。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-109">These concepts provide the basis for quantum algorithms that harness the power of quantum computing to solve complex problems.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-110">本文會說明量子運算所依據的一些量子力學基本概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-110">This article describes some of the essential concepts of quantum mechanics on which quantum computing is based.</span></span>

## <a name="a-birds-eye-view-of-quantum-mechanics"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-111">縱觀量子力學</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-111">A bird’s-eye view of quantum mechanics</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-112">量子力學 (也稱為量子理論) 是物理學的一個分支，可處理原子和次原子層級的粒子。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-112">Quantum mechanics, also called quantum theory, is a branch of physics that deals with particles at the atomic and subatomic levels.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-113">不過，在量子層級中，許多理所當然的力學定律並不適用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-113">At the quantum level, however, many of the laws of mechanics you take for granted don’t apply.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-114">疊加、量子測量和糾纏是量子運算的三大現象。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-114">Superposition, quantum measurement, and entanglement are three phenomena that are central to quantum computing.</span></span>  

### <a name="superposition-vs-binary-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-115">疊加與二進位運算的比較</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-115">Superposition vs. binary computing</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-116">想像一下，您正在客廳運動。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-116">Imagine that you are exercising in your living room.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-117">您先把身體往左轉到底，然後再往右轉到底。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-117">You turn all the way to your left and then all the way to your right.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-118">現在，同時往左和往右轉到底。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-118">Now turn to your left and your right at the same time.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-119">您不可能做到這一點 (至少不把自己切成兩半是辦不到的)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-119">You can’t do it (not without splitting yourself in two, at least).</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="6462a-120">很明顯地，您無法同時處於這兩種狀態，您不可能同時間既面向左邊又面向右邊。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-120">Obviously, you can’t be in both of those states at once – you can’t be facing left and facing right at the same time.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-121">不過，如果您是量子粒子，由於有稱為**疊加** (也稱為**相干性**) 的現象，您會有一定的概率*面向左邊*以及一定的概率*面向右邊*。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-121">However, if you are a quantum particle, then you can have a certain probability of *facing left* AND a certain probability of *facing right* due to a phenomenon known as **superposition** (also known as **coherence**).</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-122">量子粒子 (例如電子) 有自己的「向左或向右」特性，例如**自旋** (稱為向上或向下)，或者，為了更加貼近傳統的二進位運算，我們可以將其說成 1 或 0。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-122">A quantum particle such as an electron has its own “facing left or facing right” properties, for example **spin**, referred to as either up or down, or to make it more relatable to classical binary computing, let’s just say 1 or 0.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-123">當量子粒子處於疊加態時，其會是無限多狀態 (介於 1 和 0 之間) 的線性組合，但在您實際觀察之前，您不會知道其處於哪一種狀態，而這又帶出了下一個現象，那就是**量子測量**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-123">When a quantum particle is in a superposition state, it’s a linear combination of an infinite number of states between 1 and 0, but you don’t know which one it will be until you actually look at it, which brings up our next phenomenon, **quantum measurement**.</span></span>

### <a name="quantum-measurement"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-124">量子測量</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-124">Quantum measurement</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-125">現在，讓我們假設您有朋友來訪，想要將您運動的樣子拍下來。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-125">Now, let’s say your friend comes over and wants to take a picture of you exercising.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-126">最有可能的情況是，他們只會拍到您往左或往右轉到底時的模糊影像。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-126">Most likely, they’ll get a blurry image of you turning somewhere between all the way left and all the way right.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-127">但如果您是一顆量子粒子，情況就會變得很有趣了。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-127">But if you’re a quantum particle, an interesting thing happens.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-128">在他們拍照時，無論您在哪裡，照片上一律會顯示您不是往左轉到底就是往右轉到底，而不會介於兩者之間。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-128">No matter where you are when they take the picture, it will always show you either all the way left or all the way right – nothing in-between.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-129">這是因為觀察或測量量子粒子的動作會讓疊加態**塌縮** (也稱為**退相干**)，而讓粒子呈現 1 或 0 的傳統二進位狀態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-129">This is because the act of observing or measuring a quantum particle **collapses** the superposition state (also known as **decoherence**) and the particle takes on a classical binary state of either 1 or 0.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-130">這種二進位狀態對我們很有用，因為在運算時，您可以使用 1 和 0 來做許多事。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-130">This binary state is helpful to us, because in computing you can do lots of things with 1’s and 0’s.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-131">不過，量子粒子一旦遭到測量並塌縮，就會永遠保持在該狀態 (就和您的照片一樣)，並且一律會是 1 或 0。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-131">However, once a quantum particle has been measured and collapsed, it stays in that state forever (just like your picture) and will always be a 1 or 0.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-132">但正如您稍後會看到的，在量子運算中，有一些運算可以讓粒子「重設」回疊加態，以便能再次用於量子運算。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-132">As you’ll see later, though, in quantum computing there are operations that can “reset” a particle back to a superposition state so it can be used for quantum calculations again.</span></span>

### <a name="entanglement"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-133">糾纏</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-133">Entanglement</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-134">最有趣的量子力學現象可能是兩個以上的量子粒子會變得彼此**糾纏**的能力了。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-134">Possibly the most interesting phenomenon of quantum mechanics is the ability of two or more quantum particles to become **entangled** with each other.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-135">粒子在變成糾纏狀態時會形成單一系統，而讓任何一個粒子的量子狀態無法與其他粒子的量子狀態分開描述。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-135">When particles become entangled, they form a single system such that the quantum state of any one particle cannot be described independently of the quantum state of the other particles.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-136">這表示不論您對某個粒子套用了什麼運算或程序，其他粒子也會受到牽連。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-136">This means that whatever operation or process you apply to one particle correlates to the other particles as well.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-137">除了這個相互依存關係外，這些粒子即使彼此相隔遙遠的距離 (甚至以光年計)，彼此間的連結也不會斷裂。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-137">In addition to this interdependency, particles can maintain this connection even when separated over incredibly large distances, even light-years.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-138">量子測量的效應也適用於糾纏的粒子，因此當某個粒子遭到測量並塌縮，另一個粒子也會塌縮。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-138">The effects of quantum measurement also apply to entangled particles, such that when one particle is measured and collapses, the other particle collapses as well.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-139">由於糾纏在一起的量子位元之間會相互關聯，因此測量某個量子位元的狀態，就會提供另一個量子位元狀態的相關資訊，這個特性對於量子運算非常有用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-139">Because there is a correlation between the entangled qubits, measuring the state of one qubit provides information about the state of the other qubit – this particular property is very helpful in quantum computing.</span></span>

### <a name="qubits-and-probability"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-140">量子位元和概率</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-140">Qubits and probability</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-141">傳統電腦會以位元儲存和處理資訊，位元的狀態可以是 1 或 0，但不會同時是兩者。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-141">Classical computers store and process information in bits, which can have a state of either 1 or 0, but never both.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-142">量子運算中所對應的則是**量子位元**，其代表量子粒子的狀態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-142">The equivalent in quantum computing is the **qubit**, which represents the state of a quantum particle.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-143">由於疊加的關係，量子位元既可以是 1 或 0，也可以是兩者之間的任何值。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-143">Because of superposition, qubits can either be 1 or 0 or anything in between.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-144">根據其設定而定，量子位元會有一定*概率*塌縮為 1 或 0。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-144">Depending on its configuration, a qubit has a certain *probability* of collapsing to 1 or 0.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-145">量子位元會塌縮向哪一方的概率則由**量子干涉**決定。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-145">The qubit's probability of collapsing one way or the other is determined by **quantum interference**.</span></span> 

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-146">還記得為您拍照的朋友嗎？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-146">Remember your friend that was taking your picture?</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-147">假設他們的相機上有特殊的濾光片，稱為*干涉*濾光片。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-147">Suppose they have special filters on their camera called *Interference* filters.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-148">如果他們選取 *70/30* 濾光片並開始拍照，則有 70% 的照片您會面向左邊，有 30% 的照片您會面向右邊。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-148">If they select the *70/30* filter and start taking pictures, in 70% of them you will be facing left, and in 30% you will be facing right.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-149">濾光片干涉了相機的正常狀態，而影響其行為的概率。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-149">The filter has interfered with the regular state of the camera to influence the probability of its behavior.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-150">同樣地，量子干涉也會影響量子位元的狀態，以影響測量期間特定結果的概率，而此概率狀態便是量子運算的擅長之處。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-150">Similarly, quantum interference affects the state of a qubit in order to influence the probability of a certain outcome during measurement, and this probabilistic state is where the power of quantum computing excels.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-151">例如，傳統電腦有兩個位元，每個位元會儲存 1 或 0，因此合起來您可以儲存四個可能值：**00**、**01**、**10** 和 **11**，但一次只會儲存其中一個。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-151">For example, with two bits in a classical computer, each bit can store 1 or 0, so together you can store four possible values – **00**, **01**, **10**, and **11** – but only one of those at a time.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-152">但對於疊加的兩個量子位元來說，每個量子位元可以是 1 或 0 或*兩者*，因此您可以同時表示相同的四個值。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-152">With two qubits in superposition, however, each qubit can be 1 or 0 or *both*, so you can represent the same four values simultaneously.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-153">如果有三個量子位元，您就可以表示八個值，有四個量子位元就可以表示 16 個值，依此類推。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-153">With three qubits, you can represent eight values, with four qubits, you can represent 16 values, and so on.</span></span>

## <a name="summary"></a><span data-ttu-id="6462a-154">摘要</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-154">Summary</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6462a-155">這些概念雖然只勾勒出了量子力學的大致輪廓，但對於量子運算而言，卻是最重要的概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-155">These concepts just scratch the surface of quantum mechanics, but are fundamentally important concepts to know for quantum computing.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="6462a-156">**疊加** - 讓量子粒子得以成為所有可能狀態組合的一種能力。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-156">**Superposition** - The ability of quantum particles to be a combination of all possible states.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="6462a-157">**量子測量** - 觀察疊加中的量子粒子，並產生其中一種可能狀態的動作。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-157">**Quantum measurement** - The act of observing a quantum particle in superposition and resulting in one of the possible states.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="6462a-158">**糾纏** - 量子粒子讓其測量結果彼此相互關聯的能力。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-158">**Entanglement** - The ability of quantum particles to correlate their measurement results with each other.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="6462a-159">**量子位元** - 量子運算中的基本資訊單位。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-159">**Qubit** - The basic unit of information in quantum computing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6462a-160">量子位元可表示所有可能狀態的疊加中量子粒子。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-160">A qubit represents a quantum particle in superposition of all possible states.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="6462a-161">**干涉** - 量子位元會因為疊加而影響其塌縮到某一方向概率的固有行為。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6462a-161">**Interference** - Intrinsic behavior of a qubit due to superposition to influence the probability of it collapsing one way or another.</span></span>
