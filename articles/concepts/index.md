# <a name="quantum-computing-history-and-background"></a><span data-ttu-id="0f037-103">量子運算的歷程和背景</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-103">Quantum computing history and background</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0f037-104">過去幾年有許多新的電腦技術問世，其中量子運算無疑是最需要開發人員這一方進行典範移轉的技術。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-104">A host of new computer technologies has emerged within the last few years, and quantum computing is arguably the technology requiring the greatest paradigm shift on the part of developers.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-105">在 1980 年代，[Richard Feynman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Feynman) 和 [Yuri Manin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuri_Manin) 提出了量子電腦的概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-105">Quantum computers were proposed in the 1980s by [Richard Feynman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Feynman) and [Yuri Manin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuri_Manin).</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-106">量子運算背後的直覺源自常被視為物理學最難堪的其中一件事：雖然科技有長足的進步，卻連簡單系統的模型都無法建立。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-106">The intuition behind quantum computing stemmed from what was data-ttu-id="0f037-109">這是因為即使模擬的是只有幾十個互相作用的粒子所形成的系統，所需的運算能力也比傳統電腦運作數千年才能提供的運算能力還要多！</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-109">This is because simulating systems of even a few dozen <span data-ttu-id="0f037-110">有許多方法可以了解為何量子力學很難模擬。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-110">There are many ways to understand why quantum mechanics is hard to simulate.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-111">最簡單的方法或許就是可以看到，量子理論可以這麼解釋，在量子層級，物質會處於許多可能的設定 (也稱為「狀態」)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-111">Perhaps the simplest is to see that quantum theory can be interpreted as saying that matter, at a quantum level, is in a multitude of possible configurations (known as *states*).</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-112">和傳統機率論不同的是，這些眾多的量子態組態 (有觀察到的可能性) 可能會彼此干擾，就和潮汐池中的波浪一樣。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-112">Unlike classical probability theory, these many configurations of the quantum state, which can be potentially observed, may interfere with each other like waves in a tide pool.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-113">此干擾讓我們無法使用統計抽樣來取得量子態的組態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-113">This interference prevents the use of statistical sampling to obtain the quantum state configurations.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-114">相反地，如果我們想要了解量子演化，就必須追蹤量子系統可能身處的「每個可能」組態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-114">Rather, we have to track *every possible* configuration a quantum system could be in if we want to understand the quantum evolution.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="0f037-115">假設有一個由電子組成的系統，這些電子可能位於 $40$ 個 (舉例數字) 位置的任何一個。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-115">Consider a system of electrons where electrons can be in any of computers.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-119">如果我們讓粒子位於 $41$ 個位置的任何一個，則組態數量會是 $2^{41}$ 的兩倍，換算下來，就會需要超過 $260$ GB 的記憶體才能儲存量子態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-119">If we allowed the particles to be in any of $41$ positions, there would be twice as many configurations at $2^{41}$ which in turn would require more than $260$ GB of memory to store the quantum state.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-120">這個增加位置數目的遊戲不可能無限地繼續下去，因為如果我們想要以傳統方式儲存量子態，我們很快就會超過全球最強大電腦的記憶體容量。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-120">This game of increasing the number of positions cannot be played indefinitely if we want to store the state conventionally as we quickly exceed memory capacities of the world's most powerful machines.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-121">在幾百個電子時，要儲存這個系統所需的記憶體會超過全宇宙的粒子數目；因此，我們的傳統電腦永遠不可能模擬出其量子動力學。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-121">At a few hundred electrons the memory required to store data-ttu-id="0f037-122">事實上，根據量子力學定律，這類系統隨時都在演變，很幸運地我們並不知道無法使用傳統運算能力來設計和模擬這類系統的演變。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-122">And yet in nature, such systems readily evolve in time <span data-ttu-id="0f037-123">這個觀察結論讓對於量子運算有先見之明的人士提出了一個簡單卻又影響重大的問題：我們能否將此一困難轉變為機會？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-123">This observation led those with an early vision of quantum computing to ask a simple yet powerful question: can we turn this difficulty into an opportunity?</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-124">具體來說，如果量子動力學是如此難以模擬，那麼當我們要建立的硬體具有和基本操作一樣的量子效果時，會發生什麼情況？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-124">Specifically, if quantum dynamics are hard to simulate what would happen if we were to build hardware that had quantum effects as fundamental operations?</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-125">我們是否可以模擬由互相作用的粒子所組成的系統，且在模擬時使用的是利用完全相同的定律來進行管理的系統？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-125">Could we simulate systems of interacting particles using a system that exploits exactly the same laws that govern them naturally?</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-126">我們是否可以調查自然界完全不存在、但遵循或受益於量子力學定律的工作？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-126">Could we investigate tasks that are entirely absent from nature, yet follow or benefit from quantum mechanical laws?</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-127">這些問題讓量子運算應運而生。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-127">These questions led to the genesis of quantum computing.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0f037-128">量子運算的基礎核心是以物質的量子態來儲存資訊，並使用量子閘操作來計算該資訊，方法是利用並學習「程式」量子干擾。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-128">The foundational core of quantum computing is to store information in quantum states of matter and to use quantum gate operations to compute on that information, by harnessing and learning to "program" quantum interference.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-129">早期有一個範例是程式設計干擾來解決被認為難以在傳統電腦上解決的問題，這是 [Peter Shor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Shor) 在 1994 年為了解決稱為「因數分解」的問題而進行的辦法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-129">An early example of programming interference to solve a problem thought to be hard on our conventional computers was done by [Peter Shor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Shor) in 1994 for a problem known as factoring.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-130">由於能夠解決因數分解的問題，因此它也能夠破壞作為現今電子商務安全性基礎的許多公開金鑰密碼系統，包括 RSA 和橢圓曲線密碼編譯。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-130">Solving factoring brings with it the ability to break many of our public key cryptosystems underlying the security of e-commerce today, including RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-131">從那時候開始，快速又有效率的量子電腦演算法就陸續開發出來，以便處理我們眾多困難的傳統工作：模擬化學、物理和材質科學中的實體系統、搜尋未排序的資料庫、解決線性方程式系統，以及機器學習。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-131">Since that time, fast and efficient quantum computer algorithms have been developed for many of our hard classical tasks: simulating physical systems in chemistry, physics, and materials science, searching an unordered database, solving systems of linear equations, and machine learning.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0f037-132">一聽到要設計量子程式來利用量子干擾，您可能會退避三舍，雖然確實很難，但我們引進了許多技術和工具 (包括我們的 Microsoft Quantum Development Kit)，來讓您更容易進行量子程式設計和和開發演算法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-132">Designing a quantum program to harness interference may sound like a daunting challenge, and while it is, many techniques and tools, including our Microsoft Quantum Development Kit, have been introduced to make quantum programming and algorithm development more accessible.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-133">有幾個基本策略可用來操作對運算有用的量子干擾，同時又不會導致解決方案迷失在混亂的量子可能性中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-133">There are a handful of basic strategies that can be used to manipulate quantum interference in a way useful for computing, while at the same time not causing the solution to be lost in a tangle of quantum possibilities.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-134">量子程式設計是一種與傳統程式設計截然不同的技巧，需要使用非常不同的工具才能了解並表達量子演算法的思維。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-134">Quantum programming is a distinct art from classical programming requiring very different tools to understand and express quantum algorithmic thinking.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-135">事實上，如果沒有一般工具來協助量子開發人員處理量子程式設計的技巧，量子演算法的開發也不會那麼容易。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-135">Indeed, without general tools to aid a quantum developer in tackling the art of quantum programming, quantum algorithmic development is not so easy.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0f037-136">我們推出了 Microsoft Quantum Development Kit 來讓日益擴大的量子社群有工具為其工作、問題和解決方案實現量子革命。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-136">We present the Microsoft Quantum Development Kit to empower a growing community with tools to unlock the quantum revolution for their tasks, problems, and solutions.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-137">我們的高階程式設計語言 Q# 是設計來解決量子資訊處理的挑戰；其已整合到軟體堆疊中，因此您可以將量子演算法編譯到量子電腦的基本操作中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-137">Our high-level programming language, Q#, was designed to address the challenges of quantum information processing; it is integrated in a software stack that enables a quantum algorithm to be compiled down to the primitive operations of a quantum computer.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0f037-138">在取得程式設計語言之前，最好先檢閱量子運算所依據的基本原理。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-138">Before approaching the programming language, it's helpful to review the basic principles on which quantum computing is based.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-139">我們會採用量子運算的基本規則來作為通則，而不是詳述其在量子力學中的基礎。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-139">We will take the fundamental rules of quantum computing to be axioms, rather than detailing their foundations in quantum mechanics.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-140">此外，我們也會假設您基本上已熟悉線性代數 (向量、矩陣等)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-140">Additionally, we will assume basic familiarity with linear algebra (vectors, matrices, and so on).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0f037-141">如果想要更深入地研究量子運算歷史和原理，建議您參閱[參考一節](xref:microsoft.quantum.more-information)，裡面會有更多資訊。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0f037-141">If a deeper study of quantum computing history and principles is desired, we refer you to the  [reference section](xref:microsoft.quantum.more-information) containing more information.</span></span>
