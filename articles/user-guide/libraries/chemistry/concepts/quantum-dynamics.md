# <a name="quantum-dynamics"></a><span data-ttu-id="43d47-103">量子 Dynamics</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-103">Quantum Dynamics</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-104">量子機制主要是配量 dynamics 的研究，它會尋求瞭解初始量子狀態 $ \ket{\psi （0）} $ 隨時間變化的方式（如需 Dirac 標記法的詳細資訊，請參閱量子計算上的[概念](xref:microsoft.quantum.concepts.dirac)檔）。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-104">Quantum mechanics is largely the study of quantum dynamics, which seeks to understand how an initial quantum state $\ket{\psi(0)}$ varies over time (see the [conceptual docs](xref:microsoft.quantum.concepts.dirac) on quantum computing for more info on Dirac notation).</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-105">具體而言，假設此初始條件為配量狀態、演進時間和量子 dynamical 系統的規格，則會搜尋量子狀態 $ \ket{\psi （t）} $。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-105">Specifically, given this initial condition a quantum state, an evolution time and a specification of the quantum dynamical system, a quantum state $\ket{\psi(t)}$ is sought.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-106">在繼續說明「配量 dynamics」之前，請先回頭執行並思考一下「傳統 dynamics」，因為它可讓您深入瞭解量子機制與傳統 dynamics 的不同之處。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-106">Before proceeding to explain quantum dynamics, it is useful to take a step back and think about classical dynamics since it provides insights into how quantum mechanics is really not that different from classical dynamics.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-107">在傳統 dynamics 中，我們從牛頓的第二個動作法則得知，物件的位置是根據 $F （x，t） = ma = m\frac {\ dd ^ 2} {\dd t ^ 2} {x} （t） $，其中 $F （x，t） $ 是 force，而 $m $ 是「大型」，而 $a $ 是加速。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-107">In classical dynamics, we know from Newton's second law of motion that the position of a particle evolves according to $F(x,t)=ma=m\frac{\dd^2}{\dd t^2}{x}(t)$, where $F(x,t)$ is the force,$m$ is the mass and $a$ is the acceleration.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-108">然後，假設有一個初始位置 $x （0） $、演進時間 $t $，以及對物件採取動作的描述，我們就可以藉由解決 $x （t） $ 的牛頓方程式所指定的差異方程式，來尋找 $x （t） $。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-108">Then, given an initial position $x(0)$, evolution time $t$, and description of the forces that act on the particle, we can then find $x(t)$ by solving the differential equation given by Newton's equations for $x(t)$.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-109">以這種方式指定強制會有點棘手。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-109">Specifying the forces in this way is a bit of a pain.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-110">因此，我們通常會根據系統的潛在能源來表達強制，這會提供 $-\ partial_x V （x，t） = m \frac{\dd ^ 2} {\dd t ^ 2} {x} （t） $。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-110">So we often express the forces in terms of the potential energy of the system, which gives us $-\partial_x V(x,t) = m \frac{\dd^2}{\dd t^2}{x}(t)$.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-111">因此，對於物件而言，系統的 dynamics 只會由可能的能源功能、物件品質和進化時間來指定。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-111">Thus, for a particle, the dynamics of the system are specified only by the potential energy function, the particle mass, and the evolution time.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-112">較廣泛的語言通常是針對超過 $F = ma $ 的傳統 dynamics 而引進。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-112">A broader language is often introduced for classical dynamics that goes beyond $F=ma$.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-113">其中一個在量子機制中特別有用的公式是 Hamiltonian 的機制。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-113">One formulation, which is particularly useful in quantum mechanics, is Hamiltonian mechanics.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-114">在 Hamiltonian 的機制中，系統的總能源和（一般化）位置和 momenta 會提供描述任意傳統物件之動作所需的所有資訊。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-114">In Hamiltonian mechanics, the total energy of a system and the (generalized) positions and momenta give all the information needed to describe the motion of an arbitrary classical object.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-115">具體而言，let $f （x，p，t） $ 是系統的通用位置 $x $ 和 momenta $p $ 的部分函式，並讓 $H （x，p，t） $ 是 Hamiltonian 函數。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-115">Specifically, let $f(x,p,t)$ be some function of the generalized positions $x$ and momenta $p$ of a system and let $H(x,p,t)$ be the Hamiltonian function.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-116">例如，如果我們採用 $f （x，p，t） = x （t） $，$H （x，p，t） = p ^ 2 （t）/2m-V （x，t） $，則我們會復原上述有關 < 牛頓 dynamics 案例。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-116">For example, if we take $f(x,p,t)= x(t)$ and $H(x,p,t)=p^2(t)/2m - V(x,t)$, then we would recover the above case of Newtonian dynamics.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-117">就通用性而言，我們會讓 \begin{align} \frac{d}{dt} f &= \ partial_t f-（\ partial_x H \ partial_p f + \ partial_p H \ partial_x f） \\ \\ & \defeq \ partial_t f + \\ {f，H \\ }。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-117">In generality, we then have that \begin{align} \frac{d}{dt} f &= \partial_t f- (\partial_x H\partial_p f + \partial_p H\partial_x f)\\\\ &\defeq \partial_t f + \\{f,H\\}.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-118">\end{align} 這裡 $ \\ {f，H \\ } $ 稱為波和8，因為它在定義 dynamics 中扮演的中央角色[，所以](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poisson_bracket)會在傳統 dynamics 中出現 ubiquitously。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-118">\end{align} Here $\\{f,H\\}$ is called the [Poisson bracket](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poisson_bracket) and appears ubiquitously in classical dynamics because of the central role it plays in defining dynamics.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-119">您可以使用完全相同的語言來描述量子 dynamics。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-119">Quantum dynamics can be described using exactly the same language.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-120">[Hamiltonian] 或 [總能源] 會完全指定任何已關閉之配量系統的動態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-120">The Hamiltonian, or total energy, completely specifies the dynamics of any closed quantum system.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-121">不過，這兩個理論之間有一些顯著的差異。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-121">There are, however, some substantial differences between the two theories.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-122">在傳統的機制中 $x $ 和 $p $ 只是數位，而在量子機制中則不是。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-122">In classical mechanics $x$ and $p$ are just numbers, whereas in quantum mechanics they are not.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-123">事實上，他們甚至不會在 $xp \ne px $。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-123">Indeed, they do not even commute: $xp \ne px$.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-124">描述這些非通勤物件的正確數學概念是一個運算子，在這種情況下，$x $ 和 $p $ 只能接受一組離散的值，以符合矩陣的概念。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-124">The right mathematical concept to describe these non-commuting objects is an operator, which in cases where $x$ and $p$ can only take a discrete set of values coincides with the concept of a matrix.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-125">因此，為了簡單起見，我們會假設我們的量子系統是有限的，因此可以使用[向量和矩陣](xref:microsoft.quantum.concepts.vectors)加以描述。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-125">Thus for simplicity, we will assume that our quantum system is finite so that it can be described using [vectors and matrices](xref:microsoft.quantum.concepts.vectors).</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-126">我們進一步要求這些矩陣是 Hermitian 的（表示矩陣的共軛轉置與原始矩陣相同）。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-126">We further require that these matrices be Hermitian (meaning that the conjugate transpose of the matrix is the same as the original matrix).</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-127">這可保證矩陣的特徵值是實值;我們強制執行的條件，以確保當我們測量的數量類似位置時，我們不會取回虛數。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-127">This guarantees that the eigenvalues of the matrices are real-valued; a condition which we impose to ensure that when we measure a quantity like position that we don't get back out an imaginary number.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-128">就像在配量機制中的位置和動力雷同必須由運算子取代，Hamiltonian 函式必須以運算子來取代。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-128">Just as the analogues of position and momentum in quantum mechanics need to be replaced by operators, the Hamiltonian function needs to be similarly replaced by an operator.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-129">例如，對於可用空間中的物件，我們有 $H （x，p） = p ^ 2/2m $，而在配量機制中，Hamiltonian operator $ \hat{H} $ 是 $ \hat{H} = \hat{p} ^ 2/2m $，其中 $ \hat{p} $ 是動量運算子。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-129">For example, for a particle in free space we have that $H(x,p) = p^2/2m$ whereas in quantum mechanics the Hamiltonian operator $\hat{H}$ is $\hat{H}= \hat{p}^2/2m$ where $\hat{p}$ is the momentum operator.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-130">從這種觀點來看，從傳統到量子 dynamics 只需要以運算子取代一般 dynamics 中所使用的變數。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-130">From this perspective, going from classical to quantum dynamics merely involves replacing the variables used in ordinary dynamics with operators.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-131">當我們透過將一般傳統 Hamiltonian 轉換成配量語言來建立 Hamiltonian 運算子之後，我們就可以透過 \begin{align} \frac{d}{dt} \hat{f} = \ partial_t \hat{f} + [\hat{f}，\hat{H}]，\end{align} （其中 $ [f，H] = fH-Hf $ 稱為 commutator）來表達任意量子機械數量（也就是配量機械運算子） $ \hat{f} （t） $ 的動態。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-131">Once we have constructed the Hamiltonian operator by translating the ordinary classical Hamiltonian over to quantum language, we can express the dynamics of an arbitrary quantum mechanical quantity (i.e. quantum mechanical operator) $\hat{f}(t)$ via \begin{align} \frac{d}{dt} \hat{f} = \partial_t \hat{f} + [\hat{f},\hat{H}], \end{align} where $[f,H] = fH -Hf$ is known as the commutator.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-132">這個運算式與上面所提供的傳統運算式完全相同，其差異在於會以 \\ \\ $f $ 和 $H $ 之間的 commutator 來取代波狀括弧 $ {f，H} $。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-132">This expression is exactly like the classical expression given above with the difference that the Poisson bracket $\\{f,H\\}$ being replaced with the commutator between $f$ and $H$.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-133">採用傳統 Hamiltonian 並使用它來尋找量子 Hamiltonian 的程式，在量子術語中稱為標準量化。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-133">This process of taking a classical Hamiltonian and using it to find a quantum Hamiltonian is known in quantum jargon as canonical quantization.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-134">我們最感興趣的是哪些運算子 $f $？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-134">What operators $f$ are we most interested in?</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="43d47-135">其答案取決於我們要解決的問題。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-135">The answer to this depends on the problem that we want to solve.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-136">可能要尋找的最實用數量是「量子狀態運算子」，如先前的概念檔中所討論的，可用來解壓縮我們想要瞭解 dynamics 的所有內容。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-136">Perhaps the most useful quantity to find is the quantum state operator, which as discussed in the earlier conceptual documentation can be used to extract everything that we would like learn about the dynamics.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-137">執行此動作之後（並將結果簡化為其中一個具有純狀態的案例），就會在 \begin{align} i \ partial_t \ket{\psi （t）} = \hat{H} （t） \ket{\psi （t）} 中找到量子狀態的 Schrödinger 方程式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-137">After doing this (and simplifying the result to the case where one has a pure state), the Schrödinger equation for the quantum state is found \begin{align} i\partial_t \ket{\psi(t)} = \hat{H}(t) \ket{\psi(t)}.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-138">\end{align}</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-138">\end{align}</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="43d47-139">這個方程式雖然可能比上述的直覺比較簡單，但也可能是瞭解如何模擬量子或傳統電腦上的量子 dynamics 的最簡單運算式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-139">This equation, though perhaps less intuitive than that given above, yields perhaps the simplest expression for understanding how to simulate quantum dynamics on either a quantum or classical computer.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-140">這是因為差異方程式的解決方案可以下列格式表示（適用于 Hamiltonian 是常數的情況，$t $） \begin{align} \ket{\psi （t）} = e ^ {-iHt} \ket{\psi （0）}。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-140">This is because the solution to the differential equation can be expressed in the following form (for the case where the Hamiltonian is constant in $t$) \begin{align} \ket{\psi(t)} = e^{-iHt}\ket{\psi(0)}.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-141">\end{align} 這裡 $e ^ {-iHt} $ 是單一矩陣。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-141">\end{align} Here $e^{-iHt}$ is a unitary matrix.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-142">這表示有一個配量線路，可以設計來執行它，因為量子電腦可以緊密地逼近任何單一矩陣。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-142">This means that there exists a quantum circuit that can be designed to perform it because quantum computers can closely approximate any unitary matrix.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="43d47-143">這種尋找配量線路以實作為時間演進運算子的動作 $e ^ {-iHt} $ 是通常稱為「量子模擬」，或特別是 dynamical 量子模擬。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="43d47-143">This act of finding a quantum circuit to implement the quantum time evolution operator $e^{-iHt}$ is what is often called quantum simulation, or in particular dynamical quantum simulation.</span></span>
